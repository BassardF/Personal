Non fiction

Elon Musk

Ashlee Vance

The interesting story of one of the most famous entrepreneur. Inspiring.

Creativity Inc

Ed Catmull

An awesome approach of risk, success and creativity. Learn about the rise of computer assisted movies and Pixar's company culture.

The magic of thinking big

David J.Schwartz

Are small goals really easier to reach than ambitious ones? David J.Schwartz may have the answer to a widespread disease: the lack of ambition. Think big and succeed.

Zero to one

Peter thiel

A sample of Petier Thiel’s knowledge of startups. Recommended by pillars of SF’s tech world such as Elon Musk, this book is full of incredibly nice tips.

4 hours workweek

Timothy Ferriss

Tim Ferriss shatters many premade ideas on success. 4 hours workweek is full of tools and tips essential to set your own goals. Give it a try if you think that yours dreams are not reachable.

4 hours body

Timothy Ferriss

A scientific approach to food management and physical training. In this book, Tim Ferris provide many “how to” which cover a broad range of goals.

Our of our minds

Ken Robinson

If you see yourself as a “suit”, unable to be creative this book is for you. Ken Robison describes creativity as something that may be learned and explain how. Don’t be shy and unleash your creativity!

Genghis Khan

Paul Ratchnevsky

Some forgotten stories are well worth discovering and this one definitively is. If you see yourself as combative and ambitious, don’t miss this biography.

Letters from a stoic

Seneca

Letters from a stoic (letters to Lucius) is an incredible guide to happiness. Embody the young Lucius and seize the opportunity to learn from one of the best-known Roman Stoic philosopher. If Seneca doesn’t fit your needs, have a try with Marcus Aurelius

Mastery

Robert Greene

Robert Greene describes how historical figures reached success through “Mastery” and explain how to get there. This book may be troubling for an all-rounder but definitively interesting.

Science fiction

Foundation

Isaac Asimov

Foundation is definitively my favourite SF cycle. Enjoy the best of Asimov’s legacy and prepare yourself to be disappointed by most of your further discoveries in the world of science fiction. Don’t forget to read "Prelude to Foundation" and "Forward the Foundation" After the cycle itself !

Dune

Frank Herbert

The legendary Dune from Frank Herbert is an awesome introduction to science fiction. Dig in a world that may boost your creativity on the long term.
